- Justification: The current analysis is technically insufficient for a high-quality economics paper because it treats a dynamic structural system as a series of disconnected, static OLS/IV regressions. This approach fails to account for the time-series persistence and endogeneity inherent in the Cobb-Douglas DGP, leading to biased parameter recovery. The proposed improvements—specifically transitioning to a System GMM framework and joint estimation of the production and capital accumulation equations—are not merely "technical layers" but are fundamental to the structural integrity of the paper. By aligning the estimation strategy with the underlying structural model, the research will move from a "correlation-based" study to a "structural-recovery" study, significantly increasing the "Acceptance Delta" for top-tier economics journals. The ROI is high because the data is already prepared; the effort is focused on rigorous econometric specification rather than data collection.
