**Title: Quantifying Endogenous Investment Dynamics and Transition Speed Modifiers**

This research characterizes the structural feedback loop between income levels and capital accumulation by estimating the elasticity of the investment rate ($s_t$) with respect to `gdp_per_capita`. By applying a dynamic panel GMM estimator, the study isolates the marginal propensity to invest as a function of current development, effectively mapping the endogenous mechanism defined in the structural model. We then test whether `trade_openness` and `government_expenditure_share` act as structural moderators that accelerate or dampen the transition speed toward steady-state capital stock ($K_{t+1}$). By interacting these policy variables with the lagged investment rate, we quantify their impact on the velocity of capital deepening, distinguishing between institutional influence and the inherent stochasticity of the production function. The analysis utilizes the fixed 0.35 capital elasticity constant as a benchmark to assess how effectively these policy levers shift countries across growth trajectories. This approach moves beyond spurious residual analysis to directly measure the empirical sensitivity of the model's endogenous investment behavior to observed macro-policy inputs.
